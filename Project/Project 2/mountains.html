Cross Island Trek

The highest point on Rarotonga

Rarotonga's rugged interior is dominated by lush mountain ranges, offering breathtaking vistas and endless opportunities for adventure. From challenging hikes to scenic drives, the island's mountainous landscapes are a true feast for the senses.

The Cross Island Trek is a must-do for those seeking an unforgettable hiking experience. This challenging yet rewarding trail takes you through dense rainforests, past cascading waterfalls, and to the island's highest point, offering panoramic views that will leave you in awe. The best part is that because no dangerous animals live on the island, you can hike early or late in the evening and still be safe.

Watch for the Cook Islands' national bird, the Kakerori, during the Cross Island hike. The Kakerori is the Rarotongan Monarch and is critically endangered. It is rare to see one of these birds, but if you do see one, you are sure to experience good luck.

Some essential facts about the Kakerori:

  • The Kakerori is a small olive-brown flycatcher found on Rarotonga.
  • The Kakerori is one of the rarest birds in the world, with only an estimated 150-300 individuals remaining in the wild as of 2022. Its population has declined dramatically due to habitat loss and predation by invasive rat species.
  • The Kakerori is considered a treasure to Cook Islanders because it is an integral part of their natural heritage. (You will see the Kakerori featured on some of the Cook Islands' rare bank notes)
  • Seeing or hearing the distinctive rattling call of the Kakerori is a one-in-a-lifetime opportunity.
