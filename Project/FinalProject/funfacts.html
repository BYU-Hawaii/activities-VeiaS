Say Hello in Cook Islands Maori!

Did you know that in the Cook Islands, people say "Kia Orana" instead of "Hello"? It means "May you live long" or "May you have life". Isn't that a lovely way to greet someone? Next time you meet a friend, try saying "Kia Orana" and share this special greeting!

The Rainbow Connection

In Cook Islands Maori, a rainbow is called "Anuanua". People believe that rainbows are bridges between the sky and the earth. Imagine sliding down a colorful rainbow bridge! What colors would you see on your way down?

Coconut Tree: The Tree of Life

In the Cook Islands, the coconut tree is very important. It's called "Nu" in Te Reo Maori. People use every part of the coconut tree - the fruit for food and drink, the leaves for baskets, and the wood for building houses! Can you think of three ways you could use a coconut?

Dancing Stories

The Cook Islands have a special dance called "Ura". It's not just fun to watch - it tells stories! Dancers use their hands, feet, and even their eyes to tell tales about nature, history, and daily life. If you could tell a story through dance, what would your story be about?
