Avarua - The Main Hub of Rarotonga

Avarua is the lively heart of Rarotonga. In the morning, the town streets wake up with busy outdoor markets. The air is filled with good smells of fresh fish, ripe fruits, and sweet flower garlands made by local crafters. As you walk through the colorful market stalls, you'll see lots of neat handmade goods. There are woven baskets, bright sarongs, and prized black pearls Rarotonga is known for. Each stall shows the rich culture of the Cook Islands. When night falls, Avarua becomes a fun place to go out. Bars and restaurants are full of people enjoying traditional island music and laughter. But Avarua is more than just shopping and eating - it's a place for adventure too! You can explore the sunken Wreck of the Maitai ship which is now an exciting dive spot. Just outside of town are ancient trails like the Ara Metua road where you can hike and see Rarotonga's wild beauty and old stories. Whether you want an exciting town visit or an outdoor adventure, Avarua has lots of fun things to experience.

Explore the sunken wreck of Maitai situated across Trader Jacks. The Maitai wreck is the perfect spot to dive and explore the misteries of the ocean. Years ago a ship traveled to the isalnd of Rarotonga but upon arrival got stuck on the reef. Nothing could be done so the crew came on island and through the years and the many storms the only thing left of the ship is the engine boiler stuck on the reef marking the beginning of the deep blue ocean.

Towards the west side of Avarua you will find the colorful, vibrant and lively Punanga Nui Market. Saturday morning to afternoon and when you will experience the most from the market. With most people off work on the weekends and every Cook Islander their own small business owner you can find foods, crafts and shows of all kinds here. One thing you cannot miss out on is the waffle shak that sells the best, crunchy waffles on the island. This breakfast meal is sure to have you craving more after the first bite. Another must have product from the market is the local crafts, hats, earrings, bags, clothes, pareaus, etc. Don't miss the opportunity to visit the iconic Punanga Nui Cultural Market, where you can immerse yourself in the local arts, music, and dance.

Avarua closes everyday at 4:30-5pm and once the sun sets the night life begins. Avarua is the main hub for night activities where you will find more than half of the night clubs on the island located. This area is filled with Raggae music from sunset to 2am Monday to Friday. The Rehab night club also provides a party bus that is a open standing bus that takes you on a drive around te island blasting music and allowing you and your friends to dance on the go.

For those that find that the night club life is not for them Avarua also has a string of christmas lights that stretch throughout the whole village of Rarotonga on the main road that gives a magical feeling. Just driving through Avarua at night gives a peaceful feeling as you are really the only people on the road while everyone else is at home or out at the club.
